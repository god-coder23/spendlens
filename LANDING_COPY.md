## FAQ

### 1. What does SpendLens actually audit?

SpendLens reviews the AI tools you already pay for, the plan each one is on, your monthly spend, seat count, and how your team uses them. It then highlights plan mismatches, possible downgrades, and cheaper ways to get similar capability.

### 2. Do I need to create an account first?

No. The audit should show value before asking for an email. You only enter an email if you want the report sent to you or want follow-up on larger savings opportunities.

### 3. How accurate are the savings estimates?

The estimates are only as good as the pricing inputs and the recommendation rules behind them. The goal is to be directionally useful and honest, not to invent fake savings where none exist.

### 4. Is this trying to push me into switching tools?

Not by default. The first job of the audit is to tell you whether you are overpaying. Sometimes the answer is a downgrade. Sometimes it is a different vendor. Sometimes it is simply that your current setup is already fine.

### 5. Why would I trust this if I already know my stack well?

Because most teams buy AI tools incrementally and rarely revisit the full stack after the first few subscriptions. SpendLens is useful as a second opinion, especially before renewals, team expansions, or procurement conversations.
